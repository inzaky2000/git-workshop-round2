ITIL, which stands for Information Technology Infrastructure Library, is a widely recognized framework for IT service management (ITSM). It provides a set of best practices and guidelines for the efficient and effective delivery of IT services.

ITIL offers a comprehensive approach to managing IT services throughout their lifecycle, from strategy and design to transition, operation, and continual improvement. The framework emphasizes aligning IT services with the needs of the business and focuses on delivering value to customers.
Key Concepts in ITIL:
1. Service Strategy: Aligning IT service management with business objectives and customer needs.
2. Service Design: Designing new or changed services and the supporting infrastructure, processes, and resources.
3. Service Transition: Managing the transition of services into the operational environment while ensuring minimal disruption.
4. Service Operation: The day-to-day management of IT services, including incident management, problem management, and service desk support.
5. Continual Service Improvement: Continuously analyzing and improving IT services to enhance their quality and efficiency.
ITIL provides organizations with a common language and a structured approach to IT service management. It helps improve communication and collaboration among different teams and departments involved in delivering IT services. ITIL also promotes a culture of continual improvement and a focus on delivering value to customers.
The framework consists of a series of publications that provide detailed guidance on implementing ITIL practices. These publications cover various aspects of IT service management and are regularly updated to reflect industry best practices.
It's important to note that while ITIL provides valuable guidance, organizations can adapt and customize the framework to suit their specific needs and requirements.
